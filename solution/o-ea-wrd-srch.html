Solution: o ea / Wrd Srch
Answer: TWO-SIDED

Written by Chris Jones

This puzzle consists of a bunch of clues that are either missing all their vowels or all their consonants. For example, the clue “Euoea o ioee Aeia i 1492 (8)” is “European who discovered America in 1492 (8)”, which is COLUMBUS. As an aid, the answers in each column are in alphabetical order, and the number at the end of each clue is the length of the answer.

The puzzle also contains two grids, one containing only consonants and the other containing only vowels, as well as a third blank grid at the bottom, with 17 lines of blanks next to it. For each of the 13 rows, the number of vowels plus the number of consonants is always equal to 13, which suggests that we can combine the rows together to make a full row of 13 letters in the bottom grid. The 17 lines of blanks next to the grid and the title suggest that it will be a word search that contains 17 words. To get these 17 words, we need to combine the vowels of the answers to the vowel-only clues with the consonants of the answers to the consonant-only clues (for example, EUIO from CENTURION and RDCTN from ERADICATION combine to form REDUCTION). This is hinted at by the title, as well as by the fact that there are both 17 vowel-only clues and 17 consonant-only clues, suggesting they can be paired up. Also, some of the consonant sequences stand out in the grid, like NHLST in the bottom row, CLKD in the top row, and SPRG down the first column. The letters at intersections of two words are highlighted above. They’re all consonants and they only lie in odd rows, and taken line by line those rows read “LK / / CN / R / N / RGMNT / GHT”. This is a final clue, and after returning vowels to it (with word breaks matching line breaks in the grid) we should parse it as “LIKE A COIN OR AN ARGUMENT (EIGHT)”. The solution to this clue is the answer to the puzzle, TWO-SIDED.
