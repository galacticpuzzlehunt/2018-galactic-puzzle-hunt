Solution: Third Rail
Answer: BONNE NUIT

Written by Charles Tam

The flavortext, title, and background graphics for the word lists all clue board game Ticket to Ride, which we confirm by searching for some of the cities listed on the tickets. But some of the cities listed, such as Boise and Cheyenne, aren’t in that game. These cities appear in Power Grid, which is also hinted by the flavortext, title, and the inclusion of certain electricity-themed words among the word lists.

The first ticket, with no missing words, clues a route from Detroit to Winnipeg. The latter only appears on the Ticket to Ride map, and the former on the Power Grid map. The insight, hinted by the flavortext, is that one must overlay the US maps from the two games and transition between them as necessary using the cities that appear in both. In this case there’s only one possible intermediate city: Duluth. This implies that MEGAWATTS maps to the 15-value Detroit-Duluth link in Power Grid, and that LEXICOLOGICALLY maps to the 4-Black Duluth-Winnipeg link in Ticket to Ride.

It is left to the solver to discover that Power Grid links use the Scrabble valuation of the word, e.g. MEGAWATTS = 3 + 1 + 2 + 1 + 4 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 = 15. Paths that testsolving teams have taken were “valuation methods that are slightly bigger than word length” and “ask Wolfram Alpha about the word”.

Ticket to Ride links, in accordance with the mechanics of the game, count the frequency of a particular letter in the associated word. For example, LEXICOLOGICALLY has 4 L’s (indicating that the letter associated with Black is L). Again, experimenting with the other tickets in the body of the puzzle will allow solvers to double-check these suspicions. The letter assignment for each train color is consistent across all routes:

R T I N A S E C L

As in Ticket to Ride, if a link allows for multiple colors, you need only satisfy one of them. Unlike in Ticket to Ride, Gray is its own color/letter (as opposed to being any single letter represented by another color, as the game mechanics would suggest).

Each of the nine longer routes may then be fully identified, with each omitted word corresponding to a link that exists on both maps. These words must obey the valuation rules for both maps. In addition, the number of question marks in place of each omitted word indicates its enumeration, and crossword clues are provided in alphabetical order by answer for further disambiguation.

  • Vengeful act – COUNTERATTACK
  • Unsophisticated people – HILLBILLIES
  • Explicit implication? – INNUENDO
  • Polarizing – IONIZING
  • Type of flag or mile – NAUTICAL
  • Gaudy or vulgar – OSTENTATIOUS
  • Those in charge of the mail – POSTMASTERS
  • Surety in reasoning – RIGOR
  • Crosses a line – TRANSGRESSES

Having done this, solver should then draw each route on a combined map. The route draws out a number, which is an index into the missing word for that route; this produces the answer, BONNE NUIT.
