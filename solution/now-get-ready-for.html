Solution: Now Get Ready For...
Answer: RAINBOW

Written by Patrick Xia
Pictures by Patrick Xia and Anderson Wang

First, from Googling (or just knowing) the title, we can find that it’s a reference to the Elf on the Shelf meme, and this is further confirmed by the “A on the B” phrases. Each image consists of a foreground object and a background object, but directly trying to rhyme them doesn’t work.

The twist is that there is always a third item which rhymes (very tightly) with the foreground image and belongs to the same set of objects as the background image (some possible break-ins include TORN DRAKES on the CORN FLAKES, SWISS SWANSON on the WISCONSIN, and CRANIUM potentially rhyming with another element). The “A on the B” phrases suggest indexing A into the foreground and B into this third item. The items and sets are shown in the following table, with the indices bolded.

Reading the bolded letters gives AUSTEN STAGS MATE for the foreground images and ANGEL BY THE WINGS for the background images. Reusing the same mechanic, we want to find something that rhymes with JANE DOE (the phrase clued by AUSTEN STAG’S MATE) and is in the same category as ANGEL BY THE WINGS. “Angel by the Wings” is a Sia single, and her only single that rhymes with JANE DOE is RAINBOW, the final answer.

Author’s Notes

PX: The puzzle was originally inspired by one of my friends who made and posted a meme involving the recursive definition of the Ackermann function on top of a picture of an on-campus building, presumably Hackerman Hall. However, he mistakenly used a picture of a different building, Mason Hall. People in the comments who understood the math part of the joke assumed it was Hackerman and liked it, while people who didn’t understand it asked for an explanation, expecting something rhyming with Mason. My goal for this puzzle was to initially invoke the same sense of confusion when solving this puzzle: “This look like Rouge on Luge. Wait, that’s not Luge!” and “How do we get this picture of an Amazon purchase to rhyme with Copper?”

This puzzle went through several iterations; one version used the set mechanic for both the foreground and background images (e.g. Roadhog on UPenn would clue Winston on Princeton because Roadhog and Winston are both Overwatch heroes and UPenn and Princeton are both Ivy League universities). However, finding reasonable pairs and creating a consistent and thematic final clue phrase were difficult. Furthermore, we thought it was less fun than the final version due to the massive amount of search that needed to be done.

Another aim of this puzzle was to be diverse in category choice by not picking the usual suspects, though ultimately, this was not as successful as I had hoped (US states, US president, mostly US culture). Some pairs that were removed were “Sour Squid/Power Grid”, “Footwalls/Sootballs” (replaced by “Let it Go”), “Blue Mango/Durango” (replaced by “Minnesota”).

AW: Patrick and I learned a lot about using GIMP while making this puzzle, and we had fun with things like making the PyTorch text refract behind the dough glasses.
