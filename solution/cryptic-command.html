Solution: Cryptic Command
Answer: MANAGER

Written by Seth Mulhall
Additional clues by Lewis Chen, Anderson Wang, Jon Schneider, and Charles Tam

Upon opening this puzzle, we are greeted with a very strange Cryptic Command, a card from Magic: the Gathering. Whereas the actual Cryptic Command is a powerful card in the game, this one allows us to change cryptic clues.

As we will discover later, each pair of modes corresponds to a specific color of mana, or numbers.

Below the Cryptic Command are 17 more cards with a letter for art and 1-3 cryptic clues each. These cryptic clues can be solved after applying two of the modes from the Cryptic Command to it, as shown below. Each answer is associated with either one of the 5 Magic colors or a number. Taken together, the clues on a card indicate the casting cost of a unique card in Magic: the Gathering (e.g., Emrakul, the Promised End has a casting cost of {13}, and no other card does). These costs can be searched for using the official MtG card database, Gatherer, or any number of unofficial MtG card search websites.

After identifying these cards, we find that the words at the bottom of the puzzle can be found in exactly one of these cards’ textboxes (e.g. “step” appears on Bearer of the Heavens and none of the other 16 cards). By replacing the word with the associated card’s letter (e.g. the first word “step” goes to C), we can translate it into the following:

Clan cod consume you (5)
Old smelly fruit missing a good start (6)
Former girlfriend island in France emit air (5)
Spirits spin menagerie yeast (4)
Appropriated affirmation in tern (5)
After time gateau loses first ten fastener (4)
Incendiary Generic Austere Generic Profane Cryptic Primal

The last 7 lines are the names of the cycle of Commands in Magic (including our very own Cryptic Command) and the word GENERIC twice. Each Command is one of the five colors, and “generic” mana is used to describe the numbers in casting costs. Since each pair of modes is associated with a color, we can assign these six final clued words to the words in the final line, giving the phrase:

DURING COULD EXILE COULD TOKEN TAKE OOZE

Replacing these words with the appropriate card’s letter once more gives the answer MANAGER.

Author’s Notes

This puzzle was inspired by the fact that certain geographical features can be used to clue colors of Magic (like using GALAPAGOS to indicate Blue). In fact, this is what a first draft of the puzzle did, but we quickly saw that using cryptics for words like WAIPOUA and OKEFENOKEE wouldn’t be fun for anyone. Even after going with shorter words, this puzzle still turned out really difficult. I hope it was still fun!

When this answer was assigned to me, the editor said “I just thought of a manager as someone who commands”. He missed the great pun in “mana”-ger, and I’m grateful this answer was assigned to this puzzle.

An initial version of this puzzle had words at the bottom, like “Stokov”, that could more easily be associated with cards. This turned out to be a bit of a disaster in testsolving when the testsolving group solved a few of the cryptics and then managed to identify all 17 cards using only the words in their boxes, eventually solving the puzzle “backwards”! The original version also didn’t provide the card to letter mapping, as we intended it to be a “cryptic-gram”, but we added those letters when we realized that the cryptogram was not that clear without having exactly 26 cards, and it wasn’t the interesting part of the puzzle anyways.
