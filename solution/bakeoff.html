Solution: The Wepp Perflontus Bake Off
Answer: GREAT PUMPKIN

Written by Nathan Pinsker and Lucy Zhang

The main challenge of this puzzle is in identifying the English recipes and the ingredients being employed. You can obtain this information in a variety of different ways: just one of them will likely not be enough to solve the puzzle. Some of these include:

  • The method of preparation of each recipe; what tools it calls for
  • The amount of each ingredient being used (baked goods use lots of flour and water, and not much salt)
  • Linguistic commonalities of the words (fruits always end in “-gom”)
  • The structure of a cookbook (dishes are separated into “Components”, “Breakfast”, “Entrees”, “Sides”, and “Desserts”)
  • Examining some of the ingredients (spices are in shakers labeled with their alien names)
  • Trial and error with the cooking interface

Below is an interactive version of the menu with a button to toggle between English and the alien language at will.



The only recipe that you’re required to cook is the final one (Ozz-Moffkay Tok-Hompliss Rortwipp Toreils, or “Oatmeal Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies”). You can cook that recipe by adding the following ingredients:

  • sugar
  • peanut butter
  • butter
  • salt
  • baking soda (will be changed to “leavening agent”)
  • eggs
  • flour
  • oats
  • chocolate

When you successfully bake a batch of cookies, the waiter will pass you a note that reads “wepp jarpagom” and will heavily imply that that’s the answer. However, submitting “wepp jarpagom” won’t work -- as you must translate the note to English as well.

If you’ve untangled the second-to-last recipe (pumpkin pie), you’ll know that jarpagom means “pumpkin”. However, the word wepp doesn’t appear in the cookbook at all — but it does appear in the puzzle’s title! The flavor text implies that perflontus means “Galactic”, meaning that “The Wepp Perflontus Bake Off” must translate to “The Great Galactic Bake Off”. Thus, the English translation of wepp jarpagom is GREAT PUMPKIN, which is the answer.

Author’s Notes

This puzzle came from the idea of writing a cooking puzzle that actually tests your understanding of cooking. It was especially attractive to us since we both enjoy cooking (Nathan had also been watching Shokugeki no Soma, an anime about cooking), and we aren’t aware of any puzzle that’s tried to do this before. We’re very happy with the end result and the warm feedback it’s received, and we hope it serves as the first step into a larger world of cooking puzzles.

The puzzle started its life as a text adventure (it was inspired partially by The Gostak, another puzzle about figuring out alien words). We soon realized it didn’t really take advantage of the text adventure format (in fact, repeatedly typing “add X” was very repetitive) and rebuilt it into the current format. Looking back, we still see some ways the repetition could have been reduced even further, such as letting solvers add multiple ingredients at once, but we hope these omissions weren’t conspicuous enough to greatly irritate anybody.

A few people asked us where the words in this puzzle came from. The answer is that Nathan just made them up, choosing words that “sounded right”.
