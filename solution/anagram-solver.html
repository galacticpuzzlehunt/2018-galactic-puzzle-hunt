Solution: Anagram Solver
Answer: ANAGRAM SOLVER

Written by Colin Lu, Jon Schneider, and Anderson Wang

Counting the letters, we find that there are 720 total letters, with 600 seemingly-random letters at the beginning and 120 letters in alphabetical order at the end. The first step is to realize, maybe with help from the title, that the 600 letters can be broken up into 120 groups of 5 letters, with each group uniquely anagramming to a common 5-letter word. These 120 words are in alphabetical order. Because there are also 120 letters at the bottom, it makes sense to pair each 5-letter word with the corresponding letter at the bottom.

These words and letters are listed in the table below, along with the “permutation number” (for example, to go from ATDMI to ADMIT, you have to take the 1st, 3rd, 4th, 5th, and 2nd letters of ATDMI in order, so the permutation number is 13452).

There are 5!=120 possible permutation numbers, and each one appears exactly once, which means we can reorder the 120 bottom letters by the permutation order of their corresponding words. Doing this gives the string: AXISHSBAEABMBLDOBMOCPOCYOWCRYEDGLFATLDOFGFORHAEZONHRUCKLMETUOTAHAPRITLPOASOPETKRWNITUTNIVAEWEIDWAEEEEFHIILLNOPRSSTTTUWZZ

This string contains 96 characters at the beginning followed by 24 alphabetized characters at the end, so we can do the same thing but with 4-letter words!

Author’s Notes

The first version of this puzzle had an initial layer of 720 6-letter anagrams followed by these two layers. We might have stuck with that if this were a Mystery Hunt puzzle (or even gone up to 5040 7-letter anagrams!), but it was pretty clear that starting at the 5-letter layer was a lot more fun to do by hand and still allowed us to include the recursive step, so it was an easy decision to change it.
