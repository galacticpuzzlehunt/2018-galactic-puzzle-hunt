Solution: Sequencing
Answer: DEOXYRIBOSE

Written by Robert Tunney

In this puzzle, we are presented with twelve DNA sequences. The first step is to notice that these sequences come in pairs, and all of the pairs have matching lengths, except for the first. In addition, all of the sequences have lengths that are multiples of three, which suggests that these sequences can be treated as a series of codons.

The next step is to translate the codons into the amino acid alphabet. Upon translation, the pairs of sequences become appreciably more similar.

The first four characters in each pair are identical, and are separated from the rest of the sequence by an I (and then a mismatched character). Reading off these characters gives us NEEDLEMAN WVNSCH ALIGNMENT. This clues the phrase NEEDLEMAN WUNSCH ALIGNMENT, with one error because U is not in the amino acid alphabet. The Needleman-Wunsch algorithm performs alignment on pairs of strings.

The next step is to apply the Needleman-Wunsch algorithm to each pair of amino acid sequences. This algorithm finds an alignment that maximizes the number of character matches, while minimizing the number of mismatches and gaps, under a scoring scheme for each of these events. We tested this alignment to be robust to a few common scoring schemes (e.g. match=1 or match=0, gap=-1, mismatch=-1), and to work with the default parameters of the Needleman-Wunsch tools provided by NCBI BLAST and EMBL-EBI EMBOSS (the most commonly used/easy to find alignment tools). It’s also possible to solve this puzzle without Needleman-Wunsch if you notice that the sequences are similar and try to align them yourself.

The next step is to notice the structure in the gaps in the alignments. They are always one character long, and alternate perfectly between the pairs of sequences, starting in the second sequence. Read off the characters opposite the gaps to get:

GACAATGCC TCAGCG TGTTGT CATGCT CGCATA GATGAA

These are all characters in the DNA alphabet. No other ACGTs appear in the amino acid sequences, except in the acrostic clue phrases. This confirms that the alignments and extractions are correct.

Finally, translate these sequences into the amino acid alphabet once more, to get:

GACAATGCC TCAGCG TGTTGT CATGCT CGCATA GATGAA
D  N  A   S  A   C  C   H  A   R  I   D  E   --> DNA SACCHARIDE

The saccharide (sugar) in DNA is DEOXYRIBOSE, which is the answer to this puzzle.
